## üìã MANAGER COACHING QUICK REFERENCE

### When to Coach
- **Pre-Shift**: Set expectations for the shift
- **During Shift**: Real-time feedback (positive reinforcement & corrections)
- **Post-Shift**: Review observations and development areas
- **Weekly 1:1s**: Deep dive on patterns and growth

### How to Coach
1. **OBSERVE**: Watch the behavior in action
2. **RECOGNIZE or REDIRECT**: Immediate feedback
3. **EXPLAIN WHY**: Connect to guest experience
4. **PRACTICE**: Role-play if needed
5. **FOLLOW-UP**: Check progress next shift

## üö™ HOST - "WELCOME ALL GUESTS TO THE PARTY"

### Observable Behaviors
‚úÖ **GREETING**
- Smiles and makes eye contact with guests within 30 seconds of entry
- Uses exact phrase: "Hi! Welcome to Chili's!"
- Enthusiastic tone (not robotic)

‚úÖ **DOOR SERVICE**
- Opens door for ALL arriving guests
- Opens door for ALL departing guests
- Assists with coats, strollers, or packages

### Coaching Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Host on Phone When Guests Enter
**WHAT YOU SEE**: Host finishing text message, guests standing at host stand for 45 seconds

**COACHING MOMENT**:
- "Hey [Name], I noticed guests waiting at the stand. What happened?"
- **TEACH**: "Our 30-second greeting creates the first impression. If you need to check your phone, step to the back for 10 seconds so you can give guests your full attention when they arrive."
- **PRACTICE**: "Let's practice: I'm a guest walking in, show me your best welcome!"

#### Scenario 2: Weak Greeting Energy
**WHAT YOU SEE**: Monotone "welcome to chili's" without smile or eye contact

**COACHING MOMENT**:
- "I heard you greet that table, but let me show you the difference..." [demonstrate enthusiastic greeting]
- **TEACH**: "Guests decide in 7 seconds if they want to be here. Your energy sets the tone for their whole experience."
- **PRACTICE**: Have them greet you 3 times with increasing energy

#### Scenario 3: Forgot to Open Door for Departing Guests
**WHAT YOU SEE**: Guests leaving, pushing door open themselves while host watches

**COACHING MOMENT**:
- "Quick reminder - we open doors coming AND going. Why do you think we do that?"
- **TEACH**: "The last impression is as important as the first. When we walk them to the door and open it, they leave feeling special."

### Recognition Examples
- "üî• [Name] just greeted 3 tables in a row with perfect energy! That's how we start every guest experience right!"

## üçΩÔ∏è SERVER - "MAKE EVERY GUEST FEEL LIKE THEY'RE A REGULAR"

### Observable Behaviors
‚úÖ **60-SECOND GREETING**
- Arrives at table within 60 seconds of seating
- Recommends a marg and app
- Takes drink order

‚úÖ **ATTENTIVENESS**
- Regular check-ins (not just 2-minute, 2-bite)
- Silent refills (fills drinks without being asked)
- Pre-bussing (clearing finished plates promptly)

### Coaching Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Late Table Greeting (Over 2 Minutes)
**WHAT YOU SEE**: Server delivering food to table 42, hasn't greeted table 43 seated 2:30 ago

**COACHING MOMENT**:
- "Table 43 just hit 2 minutes. What's your plan?"
- **TEACH**: "I know you're delivering 42 right now. Quick fix: Eye contact with 43, mouth 'I'll be right there!' That acknowledges them while you finish this task."
- **ACTION**: "After you drop 42, go straight to 43. If this happens again, flag me or another server for a courtesy greeting."

#### Scenario 2: No Recommendation Given
**WHAT YOU SEE**: Server greets, asks "What can I get started for you?" without suggesting anything

**COACHING MOMENT**:
- "Great greeting speed! I want you to add one thing - a specific recommendation. Why do you think that matters?"
- **TEACH**: "Guests want to be told what's great, not figure it out. When you say 'The Watermelon Marg and Texas Cheese Fries are amazing today,' you make them feel like a regular who knows the good stuff."
- **PRACTICE**: "Greet me right now with your favorite marg and app combo."

#### Scenario 3: Drinks Empty at Table
**WHAT YOU SEE**: Walking past table with two empty Coke glasses

**COACHING MOMENT** (Real-time):
- [Quietly] "Table 25 needs refills - grab those now."
- [Follow-up later]: "What helps you catch empty drinks before guests ask?"
- **TEACH**: "Every time you pass your section, scan for empties. Silent refills make guests feel taken care of without saying a word."

#### Scenario 4: Strong Performance
**WHAT YOU SEE**: Server refills drinks, clears finished appetizer plate, checks on food quality, all in one smooth visit

**COACHING MOMENT**:
- "Stop right there - that was textbook table touch. You refilled, pre-bussed, and quality-checked in 30 seconds. THAT is making them feel like regulars. Keep that up!"

### Recognition Examples
- "üî• Shoutout to [Name] - just watched them turn a 4-top into regulars with their recommendations and energy!"
- "Table 27 just asked for [Name] by name for their next visit. That's the power of attentiveness!"

## üí° COACHING TIPS

### DO:
‚úÖ Coach in private, praise in public
‚úÖ Focus on behavior, not person ("The greeting was late" not "You're slow")
‚úÖ Ask questions first: "What happened?" "What's your plan?"
‚úÖ Role-play new behaviors immediately
‚úÖ Follow up next shift

### DON'T:
‚ùå Wait until after shift to address issues
‚ùå Coach in front of guests
‚ùå Assume they know why it matters
‚ùå Give feedback without solutions
‚ùå Forget to recognize when they improve

## üéì TRAINING NEW TEAM MEMBERS

### Week 1: Shadow & Learn
- Walk them through the behavior board
- Show them what "great" looks like
- Let them observe excellent team members

### Week 2: Practice with Feedback
- Have them execute behaviors with you watching

### Week 3: Independence with Check-Ins
- Let them work independently
- Spot-check behaviors throughout shift
- Weekly development conversation

### Week 4: Mastery & Mentorship
- Assess full capability
- Pair them with newer team members to teach
- Set new development goals
