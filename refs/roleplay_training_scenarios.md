# üé≠ ChiliHead Hospitality Behaviors - Role-Play Training Scenarios

## Purpose
Use these role-play exercises during pre-shift meetings, training sessions, or 1:1 coaching to practice and reinforce ChiliHead Hospitality Behaviors.

---

## üìã HOW TO USE THESE SCENARIOS

### Setup (2 minutes)
1. Choose a scenario relevant to your team's current development needs
2. Assign roles: Manager plays guest, team member plays their position
3. Explain the scenario context

### Execute (3 minutes)
1. Run through the scenario
2. Let the team member respond naturally
3. Don't interrupt - observe the full interaction

### Debrief (5 minutes)
1. Ask: "How did that feel? What went well?"
2. Provide specific feedback: "I loved when you..."
3. Identify one improvement opportunity
4. Run it again with the improvement

### Repeat (3 minutes)
1. Switch roles if time allows
2. Let another team member try
3. Have top performers demonstrate

## üö™ HOST SCENARIOS

### Scenario 1: THE RUSH - Multiple Parties Arriving

**CONTEXT:**
It's Friday at 6:45 PM. You have 3 parties that just walked in within 30 seconds of each other: a couple, a family of 5, and a group of 4 friends. You're alone at the host stand.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** All three parties (rotate attention between them)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does host acknowledge ALL parties quickly?
- Exact greeting: "Hi! Welcome to Chili's!"
- Does host open the door for arriving guests?
- How does host manage multiple greetings?
- Energy level under pressure

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "When you're slammed, eye contact + 'I'll be right with you!' lets everyone know you see them"
- "Greet in order of arrival, but acknowledge everyone in the first 30 seconds"
- Practice the rapid greeting: Party 1 (full greeting) ‚Üí Party 2 & 3 (acknowledge) ‚Üí Return to Party 1 (seat)

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
"Hi! Welcome to Chili's!" [to couple] "Welcome in! I'll be right with you!" [to family] "Be right there!" [to group of 4] ‚Üí Seats couple ‚Üí Returns to family with full energy

---

### Scenario 2: THE DISTRACTION - Phone Rings While Greeting

**CONTEXT:**
You're greeting a party of 2 when the phone starts ringing. There are 2 other parties waiting to be seated, and a to-go order is at the stand ready for pickup.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** The party being greeted + phone caller (ring loudly)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does host finish greeting before addressing phone?
- How does host prioritize?
- Does host maintain composure?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Guests in front of you come first. Phone can wait 30 seconds."
- "If you MUST answer, say 'Please hold one moment' immediately, finish your greeting, then take the call"
- "Or better: Let another team member grab the phone"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
Continues greeting without breaking eye contact with guests ‚Üí Seats them ‚Üí THEN addresses phone or to-go

---

### Scenario 3: THE DEPARTURE - Busy and Guests Leaving

**CONTEXT:**
You're entering a party into the system when you notice a family of 4 is approaching the door to leave. The dining room is busy.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** The departing family

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does host notice them leaving?
- Does host open the door?
- What does host say?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Last impression = first impression. Stop what you're doing."
- "Walk to door, open it, say 'Thanks for coming in! See you next time!'"
- "If you can't get there, at least make eye contact and wave"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
Looks up from system ‚Üí "Thanks so much for coming!" ‚Üí Walks to door ‚Üí Opens door ‚Üí "See you soon!"

### Scenario 4: THE LATE GREETING - Juggling Multiple Tables

**CONTEXT:**
You just sat a new table (Table 25). You're currently at Table 22 taking an order, Table 23 needs drinks delivered, and Table 24's food just came up. It's been 45 seconds since Table 25 was seated.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Table 25 (looking around expectantly)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does server acknowledge Table 25 before 60 seconds?
- How does server communicate delay if needed?
- What's the verbal/non-verbal acknowledgment?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Eye contact + hand signal buys you 30 seconds"
- "Or: Mouth 'I'll be right there!' while finishing Table 22"
- "If another server is nearby, ask for a courtesy greeting"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[While taking Table 22 order] Makes eye contact with Table 25 ‚Üí Smiles ‚Üí Holds up "one minute" finger ‚Üí Finishes 22 ‚Üí Goes straight to 25: "Hi! So sorry for the wait - welcome in!"

---

### Scenario 5: THE RECOMMENDATION - No Clue What They Want

**CONTEXT:**
You approach a table of 2. They've been looking at menus but seem overwhelmed. You deliver your 60-second greeting.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Indecisive couple: "Umm...we're not sure yet. What's good?"

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does server give specific recommendation?
- Marg AND app suggested?
- Confidence in delivery?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Never say 'everything's good' - that's not helpful"
- "Pick YOUR favorites: 'I love the [Marg] and [App] - they're amazing!'"
- "Sound excited about it. If you don't sell it, they won't buy it"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
"Okay so here's what I'd do: Start with the Watermelon Marg - it's sweet and refreshing - and the Texas Cheese Fries with ranch. Honestly my favorite combo. Can I grab those for you while you decide on entrees?"

---

### Scenario 6: THE REFILL FAIL - Empty Drinks Unnoticed

**CONTEXT:**
You're walking back from the kitchen to your section. You pass Table 28, which has two empty Coke glasses sitting there. The guests are mid-conversation.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Guests at Table 28 (don't say anything, just sit with empty glasses)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does server notice empties?
- Does server proactively refill without being asked?
- What does server say?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Silent refills = you're attentive and they don't have to ask"
- "Every time you walk past your section, scan for empties"
- "Grab them, refill, return - takes 45 seconds and makes them feel like regulars"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[Notices empties] "Let me grab you fresh Cokes!" [Takes glasses] [Returns 45 seconds later] "Here you go!" [Doesn't wait for acknowledgment, continues moving]

---

### Scenario 7: THE PRE-BUS - Finished Appetizer Plate

**CONTEXT:**
You're checking on Table 30's entrees. You notice their appetizer plate is empty and pushed to the side of the table.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Guests: "Everything's great, thank you!"

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does server clear the finished app plate?
- How smoothly is it done?
- Does server ask permission?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Pre-bussing = clean table = elevated experience"
- "You don't need to ask every time - if it's clearly done, grab it"
- "Make it part of every table touch: Refill, clear, quality check"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
"Awesome! Glad you're enjoying." [Picks up empty app plate while talking] "I'll get this out of your way. Need anything else?" [Already holding plate, keeps conversation flowing]

## üèÉ RUNNER SCENARIOS

### Scenario 8: THE MAGIC QUESTION - Delivering Food

**CONTEXT:**
You're running two entrees to Table 35. The guests look happy. You set down the plates.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Guests: [Nod and smile]

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- What does runner say when delivering?
- Does runner ask the magic question: "What more can I get you?"
- Does runner wait for a response?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Never silent drop - every table touch is an opportunity"
- "The magic question prevents them from waiting for their server"
- "Common answers: ranch, refills, napkins - you can grab all that"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[Sets down plates] "Here are your burgers! What more can I get you?" [Waits 2 seconds] Guest: "Could we get some ranch?" Runner: "Absolutely! Be right back!"

### Scenario 9: THE DRINK PRIORITY - Balancing Tasks

**CONTEXT:**
You see 3 drinks sitting in the well (ready for 2 minutes). Table 40 also has a bunch of empty plates that need clearing. Both need attention.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** The situation (set up drinks and dirty table)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- What does runner prioritize?
- Speed of decision?
- Communication with team?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Drinks before dishes, always"
- "Guests notice drink wait time more than anything"
- "Run drinks first, then circle back to pre-bus"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[Sees both tasks] "Drinks first." [Grabs drinks, delivers to table in 30 seconds] [Returns, grabs bus tub, clears Table 40]

**CONTEXT:**

### Scenario 12: THE DIRTY TABLE - Guests Just Left

**CONTEXT:**
You see a 4-top where guests just stood up and walked to the host stand to pay. The table has plates, glasses, and trash. You're currently wiping down another table.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** The situation (dirty table sitting)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- How quickly does busser notice?
- Does busser finish current task or pivot?
- Does busser communicate with host about table status?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Dirty tables cost us money - every minute = lost chance to seat"
- "Finish what you're doing (10 seconds) then hit the dirty table"
- "Always radio the host: 'Table 42 clear and clean, ready to seat'"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[Finishes wiping current table] [Walks to dirty 4-top with bus tub] [Clears table in 60 seconds] [Wipes AND spot sweeps] [Radios:] "Table 42 ready to seat!"

**CONTEXT:**
You're in the kitchen putting dirty dishes in the pit. Your headset crackles: "Hey busser, is Table 38 clean yet? I have a party ready."

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Host on headset (urgency in voice)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- How quickly does busser respond?
- What does busser say?
- Does busser follow through?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Quick response = host can make decisions"

- "If it's not clean, give an ETA: 'I'll have it ready in 2 minutes'"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[Immediately responds] "Table 38 is clear but I haven't wiped it yet. Give me 90 seconds and it's ready!" [Drops dishes, goes to Table 38, cleans in 90 seconds, radios:] "Table 38 ready!"

## üì¶ TO-GO SCENARIOS

### Scenario 14: THE MISSING ITEM - Guest Checking Order

**CONTEXT:**
A guest arrives to pick up their order. You hand them the bag. They open it at the counter and start looking through it: "Wait, I don't see my side of ranch..."

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Guest: "I ordered extra ranch but I don't see it."

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- How does to-go specialist respond?
- Speed of resolution?
- Tone and apology?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Own it immediately: 'I'm so sorry, let me grab that for you right now'"
- "Check the bag chit: Was it on there? If yes = bagging issue. If no = order entry issue"
- "Fix it in 30 seconds and thank them for catching it"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
"Oh I'm so sorry! Let me grab that ranch for you right now." [Checks bag chit] "Looks like it got missed - my bad. Here you go, and thanks for catching that!" [Hands ranch with smile]

### Scenario 15: THE PERFECT PICKUP - Simple Order

**CONTEXT:**
A guest walks in. You have their to-go order ready at the stand. It's complete and correct.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** Guest: [Walks up to counter]

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**

- Does specialist work the bag chit?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Lead with the welcome, THEN confirm the order"
- "Work the bag chit: 'I have your two burgers, fries, and drinks - all set!'"
- "Make them feel like a guest, not a transaction"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
"Hi! Welcome to Chili's! Order for Smith?" [Guest nods] "Perfect! I have your two Oldtimers with fries, both with everything, and two Cokes. Sauces and utensils are in the bag. You're all set!" [Hands bag with smile] "Thanks for calling Chili's!"

### Scenario 16: THE SPEC CHECK - Wrong Temperature

**CONTEXT (QA):**
An order comes up in the window: Burger ordered medium-well. You're about to call for the server but something looks off. You cut into it to check - it's rare.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** The cook who made it (standing at grill)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does QA catch it before sending?
- How does QA communicate with HOH?
- What's the tone?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Every plate = eye test. If you're not sure, verify"
- "Communicate calmly: 'Hey this burger needs to go back - ticket says MW but it's rare'"
- "Thank them for the quick refire"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[Cuts burger] "Hey [Cook name], this one needs to go back - ticket says medium-well but it's rare. Can you refire?" Cook: "Yep, two minutes!" QA: "Thanks!"

### Scenario 17: THE PROACTIVE UPDATE - Order Running Behind

**CONTEXT (QA):**
You notice a large to-go order is taking longer than expected. It's been 8 minutes and it usually takes 6. The guest is expected to arrive in 2 minutes.

**MANAGER PLAYS:** To-Go specialist (not aware of delay yet)

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does QA communicate proactively?
- Does QA give an ETA?
- Does QA alert the right people?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "Proactive communication prevents problems"
- "Give specific updates: 'Table 50 to-go will be 4 more minutes - guest is expected now'"
- "Alert to-go specialist AND manager so they can communicate to guest"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
[Radios to-go] "Hey, the Smith to-go order is running about 3 minutes behind - should be ready at 6:18. Want me to call them if they arrive early?" To-go: "Yes please, thanks for the heads up!"

## üéØ MULTI-POSITION SCENARIOS

### Scenario 18: THE TEAM EFFORT - Busy Friday Night

**CONTEXT:**
It's 7:30 PM on Friday. The restaurant is at capacity. A server's drink order has been sitting in the well for 90 seconds because the server is stuck at a table with a complicated order. A runner notices.

**ROLES:**
- Manager plays: The busy server
- Team member 1: Runner
- Team member 2: Another server nearby

**WHAT TO OBSERVE:**
- Does anyone step in to help?
- How is teamwork communicated?
- What's the end result?

**COACHING FOCUS:**
- "We all own the guest experience - not just our position"
- "If you see drinks sitting, run them. If you see a table needs bussing, bus it"
- "Teamwork = everyone wins"

**EXCELLENT LOOKS LIKE:**
Runner: [Sees drinks] "Those are for Table 25, right?" [Grabs them, delivers] "Here are your drinks! [Server name] will be right over!" Server: [Returns from table] "Thanks for running those!" Runner: "No problem!"

## üìù POST-ROLE-PLAY DEBRIEF TEMPLATE

**Use this structure after each scenario:**

### 1. Self-Assessment (30 seconds)
"How do you think that went? What felt good? What felt awkward?"

### 2. Positive Reinforcement (30 seconds)
"Here's what you did really well: [specific behavior]. That's exactly what we're looking for."

### 3. One Improvement (30 seconds)
"One thing to level up: [specific behavior]. Here's why it matters: [guest impact]."

### 4. Practice the Improvement (1 minute)
"Let's run it again with that one change."

### 5. Commit to Action (30 seconds)
"What will you focus on during your next shift?"

## üèÜ GAMIFYING ROLE-PLAY TRAINING

### Make it Fun:
- **Role Reversal:** Have the best performer play the "bad" example, then the "great" example
- **Speed Rounds:** 60-second scenarios, rapid-fire feedback
- **Team Competition:** Host vs Server vs Runner - who executes best?
- **Guest Judge:** Have another team member play "mystery guest" and rate the interaction

### Recognition:
- "Role-Play Champion of the Week" shoutout
- Winner picks the next scenario topic
- Post results on Connection Board
